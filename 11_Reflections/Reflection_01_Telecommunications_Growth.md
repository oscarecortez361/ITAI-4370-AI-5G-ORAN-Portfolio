# Reflection 1: How My Understanding of Telecommunications Evolved

At the beginning of this course, I mainly thought of telecommunications as phone calls, internet service, and sending information from one place to another. I understood the general idea, but I did not fully understand how many systems and decisions are involved before information reaches the user.

The early assignments helped me build a stronger foundation. I learned how a communication system can be divided into the source, transmitter, channel, receiver, and destination. I also learned how bandwidth, latency, noise, throughput, and signal quality affect performance. The BPSK laboratory made these concepts more practical because I could see how binary information was converted into a signal, affected by noise, and then recovered by the receiver.

The RF propagation laboratory also changed how I thought about wireless coverage. The Free Space Path Loss model showed that signal loss increases as distance increases. This helped me understand that reliable wireless service depends on more than just having a strong transmitter.

Later in the course, I learned that 5G is not simply a faster version of 4G. The 5G Core, network slicing, Multi-access Edge Computing, virtualization, and service discovery make the network more flexible and programmable. Open RAN also showed me how network functions can be separated and controlled through software.

Overall, my understanding progressed from basic signal transmission to viewing telecommunications as a complete system that combines radio technology, software, cloud computing, edge resources, automation, security, and artificial intelligence.
