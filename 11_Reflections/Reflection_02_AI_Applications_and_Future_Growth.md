# Reflection 2: Applying AI to Telecommunications and Future Growth

This course helped me understand how artificial intelligence can be used to solve real telecommunications problems. Before this course, I mainly viewed AI as something used for robotics, computer vision, or general data analysis. I now understand that AI can also help communication networks predict demand, allocate resources, detect failures, reduce congestion, and improve reliability.

The network traffic prediction laboratory was one of the strongest examples. I compared ARIMA, Linear Regression, and LSTM models using simulated hourly network traffic. The results showed that model complexity does not always guarantee better performance. In this project, the feature-based Linear Regression model performed well because it used time, lag, and moving-average information that matched the repeating structure of the data. This taught me the importance of feature engineering, evaluation metrics, and selecting a model based on the problem rather than choosing the most advanced method automatically.

The Edge Computing laboratory also helped me understand the limits of deploying AI models in real systems. I worked with structured pruning, knowledge distillation, TensorFlow Lite, and INT8 quantization. These methods reduced model size and supported faster inference on a resource-constrained device. This showed me that a useful model must be accurate, efficient, and practical to deploy.

My EMS background also helped me connect these concepts to real situations. Network slicing, edge computing, traffic prioritization, and predictive systems could support patient telemetry, video communication, location services, and public-safety traffic during emergencies. In those situations, reliability and latency can directly affect care.

The main skills I improved were Python programming, machine learning, time-series analysis, model evaluation, data visualization, edge AI, and technical documentation. My future growth areas include working with real network telemetry, learning more about O-RAN xApps and rApps, improving deep-learning models, and gaining more experience with live 5G and edge platforms.

This course showed me that AI is not separate from telecommunications. It can become part of how the network observes conditions, makes decisions, and adapts to changing demand.
